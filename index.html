Hello! My name is Ana Oldham.

She/Her/Hers.

I am a certified EMT and currently working as an aide at Boulder Community Health Hospital. My current medical interests are pediatrics, infectious diseases, neurology, and psychology. I am also a Technology, Arts, and Media minor, through which I can pursue my passion for digital arts and expand my mediums for artistic expression. I enjoy character building and world building the most, and my interests lean heavily towards science fiction. Most recently, 3D modeling and novel writing are my favorite creative outlets.

3D Models

Zinkoidian

3D model, Autodesk Maya

This is the current version of the zinkoidian creature, envisioned as a human / feline hybrid. Note the hourglass tapering of the body for the female model. The face blends human and bestial characteristics, opting for an elongated animal muzzle and human eyes with prominant brow ridges. This model is in the standard "T-pose", optimizing it for further rigging and animation.

Vixle

3D model, Autodesk Maya

This model was designed as a birthday present for a friend. The creature form mixes flight and aquatic aesthetics, featuring four wings and a paddle-like tail on a long, slender body. Note that the wings were modeled with individual feathers. This model was later 3D-printed with a wood-based filament.

Animations/Videos

"Down" - An Animation

3D animation, Autodesk Maya, Hitfilm Express, Audacity

One of my first full-length animations as a solo animator, this video reflects on a dificult stage of my life, and the making of this video was partially intended to have a cathartic effect. I chose to leave this video without a happy ending, as this reflected my actual reality at the time. Instead of experiencing a "happy ending", this animation look at accepting failure and the pain that must be endured for acceptance to happen.

Coffee

3D animation, Autodesk Maya, Hitfilm Express

What had been intended as a quick mini-animation for a professor quickly became a month-long project with the addition of the fur detailing. Each strand of hair was rendered seperately, causing the video rendering to lag and causing multiple crashes. While the fur does look detailed and realistic, it has a long way to go, inspiring further projects - which are still under development - to develop a better texture for the model. Curiously, with the fur texture in place, the model was often recieved as a bear or different animal, prompting further projects to re-model the character into a more feline-like figure.

The Scariest Minute

Stop Motion Animation, Photoshop

This animation recounts a traumatic event in my life in which I was involved in a serious car crash. This is my first stop-motion animation, and was hand-drawn at 10 frames per second. Note how this fps and hand-drawn style creates a jumpy, choppy video. This effect, along with the use of white outlines on a black background, create a tone of disorientation and danger.

Books/Writing

Nadiline's Story

book, Microsoft Word, Photoshop

The first book I ever wrote, this story focusses on perservearance and resilience, as well as how one experiences physical and mental trauma. Note that the illustration is hand-drawn and colorized, which was later cleaned up using Photoshop.

Published and printed version

Rookie

book, Microsoft Word, Illustrator

The prequel to Nadiline's story, this book looks back into the main character's origins. Where Nadiline's Story looks at the use and effect of perservearance, Rookie looks at the journey to build perservearance and resistance. Note that the cover is digitaly illustrated instead of hand-drawn, to improve the quality of the image and create a more metalic texture.

Published and printed version

The Little Angel

short story, Microsoft Word, Illustrator

Diverging from young adult novels, this book is writen for young children, and describes the relationship between a dove and an angel. This piece was writen as a Mother's Day gift, and contains strong messages of love, nuturing, and growth. The book is interactive, containing places for children to draw their own perseptions of what the story looks like and encouraging them to think deeper about the book's main topics.

Published and printed version

A Tale of Two Mothers

short story, Microsoft Word, Illustrator

As with The Little Angel, this book was writen as a Mother's Day gift. It contains two different short stories, each of which reflect on the positive impact mothers have on their children. Each story comes with its own illustration. Note how both illustrations share the same style, which relies on simplification of shapes and shadows.

Published and printed version
